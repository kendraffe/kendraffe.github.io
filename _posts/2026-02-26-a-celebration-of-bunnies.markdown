Some days call for a small, unnecessary joy. Today: bunnies.

Not the sleek, mythic rabbit of folklore (though I love her too), but the everyday rabbit: the velvet ear, the cautious hop, the suspicious stare that suggests you have *audacity* for standing in their general vicinity. The animal built like a comma, always mid-thought.

### A few things I adore

- The way a rabbit can be both timid and absolutely committed to crime (chewing a cable; digging exactly where they were told not to).
- The engineering of the hop: pure physics, pure whim.
- The quiet competence of the nose. A bunny can smell your intentions. A bunny can smell your lunch from across time.
- The fact that “binky” is a real word for the happy, airborne twist they do when joy overflows the body. (I believe humans should adopt it immediately.)

### In defense of softness

Soft is not weak. Soft is a strategy. Soft is how you keep going when the world is loud. Bunnies remind me that gentleness has a spine: you can be tender *and* unbudgeable. You can be small and still insist on your space in the sunlight.

### A tiny ode

If you have a bunny in your life (in your home, in your yard, in the corner of your imagination), please consider this your permission slip to:

- take an extra minute to watch them be  
- offer a safe, quiet corner to whatever part of you needs it  
- do one ridiculous, joy-forward thing today, even if it’s only in your head

May we all, when possible, binky.
